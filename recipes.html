Recipes

We have acquired recipes from some of Ann Arbor's favorite college bars! These drinks will definitely remind you of a Saturday night on the town.

Alley Bar

Originally opened in 1880 as Charles Binder’s Saloon, Ann Arbor’s Alley Bar is a staple for both Ann Arbor residents and visitors. As one of the oldest bars in the town, you’re experiencing pieces of Ann Arbor history while enjoying a variety of mixed beverages and drinks. We’re aiming to recreate some of the classic drinks you can find at our favorite hole-in-the-wall hangout spot.

Blue Leprechaun

Established in 2008, The Blue Leprechaun is located at the heart of the University of Michigan’s campus. Offering Irish & American food in this lively pub featuring 3 bars, lots of TVs & karaoke.

The Brown Jug

This restaurant and bar opened its doors in 1936 and is a staple of Ann Arbor. They get their name from the Michigan vs Minnesota football trophy, which is also the oldest trophy in college football. Enjoy various types of foods and drinks while being surrounded by Michigan football memorabilia covering the walls.

Bo Blueberry

Hail Pomegranate

Casa Dominick's

Casa Dominick's is a cozy spot for traditional Italian eats & pizza with cocktails in Mason jars & a patio with fountain. It is close to the Law Quad, and is a great spot for people to eat and drink outside in the summer.

Sangria

Cantina

Recently renovated in 2017, Cantina is a nice spot to eat and drink with friends. They are known for their lively bar & grill offering Mexican fare plus late-night dancing with DJs & live musicians. Not only is it a restaurant but also gives you a club-like setting at night.

Frozen Margarita

Frita Batidos

Frita Batidos is a casual restaurant inspired by Cuban culture that combines a fusion of Cuban traditional elements- the frita and the batido. With all of their ingredients sourced from local farmers, Frita Batidos is a restaurant that most Ann Arbor residents know. Their batido with rum and coconut is one of a kind and is a must try for anyone visiting the city.

Garage Bar

This iconic bar, which is connected to Pizza House offers 32 craft beers on tap, countless TVs, and a full food menu from Pizza House. Being a semi outdoor bar you are able to enjoy an open air atmosphere that keeps your nights hot and drinks cold!

Good Time Charley's

Opened in 1979 and quickly became a hub for faculty and students alike. It is a casual indoor-outdoor canteen with multiple TVs, daily specials, karaoke & trivia nights. They boast their huge outdoor patio, hand-rolled Styx & Twists, Long Island fishbowls, great burgers and fries, and the original "Shooter List".

Heidelberg

Located closer to downtown Ann Arbor, Heidelberg has been serving Ann Arbor since 1961. They are a Charming 3-floor pub & eatery offers a huge, boot-shaped beer glass & ample German & American menus. The third floor also doubles as a club with DJs, live music, and dancing.

The Last Word

The last word is a speakeasy-themed bar that is a hidden gem well kept by some of Ann Arbor’s most enthusiastic bar goers. With an expansive drink and food menu and live music, the Last Word is the perfect place to go if you’re looking for something more lowkey while enjoying high quality drinks.

The Raven's Club

After opening their doors in 2011, The Ravens Club is a favorite night spot in downtown Ann Arbor. They offer seasonal food and cocktails, and have over 150 American whiskeys. They also boast their local Michigan craft beers on tap in their speakeasy style joint!

Rick's

Rick’s is arguably one of the most well known bars among Ann Arbor residents. A common place for students from the University of Michigan to gather, it’s a place that rings nostalgic for many alumni of the University. Memories are both made and lost at Rick’s and serves as an integral aspect of the student body experience at Ann Arbor.
